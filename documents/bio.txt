Raaz Dwivedi is an Assistant Professor at Cornell Tech and a field member of Computer Science, Operations Research and Information Engineering, Statistics and Applied Math, at Cornell University. He spent two years as a FODSI postdoc fellow at Harvard and MIT LIDS, and spent a summer at Microsoft Research New England. He received his Ph. D. at UC Berkeley EECS in 2021 and bachelors in EE at IIT Bombay in 2014. His research builds statistically and computationally efficient strategies for personalized decision-making with theory and methods spanning the areas of causal inference, reinforcement learning, and distribution compression. He has won the Blackwell-Rosenbluth award, a best student paper award for work on optimal compression and teaching awards at Harvard and UC Berkeley, and the President of India Gold Medal at IIT Bombay.
